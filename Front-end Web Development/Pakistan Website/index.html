Overview

About Pakistan

Pakistan, officially the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, is a country in South Asia. It is the world's fifth-most populous country, with over 230 million people — the second-largest Muslim-majority country after Indonesia.

Pakistan is bordered by India to the east, Afghanistan to the northwest, Iran to the west, and China to the northeast. To the south, it has a coastline along the Arabian Sea and the Gulf of Oman.

"A land of stunning contrasts — from the K2 summit to the Indus delta, from ancient Mohenjo-daro to a bold modern identity."

Pakistan's terrain ranges from the towering Karakoram and Himalayan ranges in the north to fertile Indus plains and the vast Thar Desert in the south. The Indus River — the country's lifeline — flows from the Himalayas to the Arabian Sea.

Geography

Map

Pakistan is located in South Asia, covering about 881,913 km² — the 33rd largest country in the world by land area. It is divided into four provinces: Punjab, Sindh, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Balochistan.

The federal capital is Islamabad. Major cities include Karachi, Lahore, Faisalabad, Rawalpindi, and Peshawar. Pakistan also has two autonomous regions: Gilgit-Baltistan and Azad Jammu and Kashmir.

Pakistan shares a 2,670 km border with Afghanistan, 3,323 km with India, 959 km with Iran, 523 km with China, and a 1,046 km coastline along the Arabian Sea.

National Symbol

Flag

The flag of Pakistan features a green field with a white crescent and star, and a white vertical stripe at the hoist. Adopted on 11 August 1947, it was designed to represent the nation's Islamic identity and the diversity of its people.

Green symbolizes prosperity and the Muslim majority. The white stripe represents all religious minorities. The crescent signifies progress, and the star denotes knowledge and light.

The flag is flown on Republic Day, Independence Day, Defence Day, and major national occasions — a constant source of pride and patriotism for Pakistanis.

National Identity

National Anthem

Pakistan's national anthem — Qaumi Taranah (قومی ترانہ) — is also known as Pak Sarzamin ("Thy Sacred Land"). Lyrics by Hafeez Jalandhari (1952), music by Ahmed G. Chagla (1949), officially adopted 16 August 1954.

The three-stanza anthem praises the sacred land, the brotherhood of the people, and the crescent and star as guides to progress and perfection.

Science & Defence

Nuclear Power

Pakistan is the first Muslim-majority country to construct and operate civil nuclear power plants. The Pakistan Atomic Energy Commission (PAEC) is solely responsible for these facilities.

Pakistan began its nuclear programme in January 1972 under Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. Today, Pakistan has five nuclear power plants (1,430 MW total) with three more under construction (3,930 MW planned).

"Pakistan's nuclear programme stands as a milestone in scientific achievement for the Muslim world."
