The Indus River is the longest and most important river in Pakistan, flowing from the Himalayas to the Arabian Sea. The river provides water for irrigation, hydroelectric power, and transportation. Pakistan is divided into four provinces: Punjab, Sindh, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Balochistan. Each province has its own capital and administrative structure. The federal capital of Pakistan is Islamabad, which is located in the Islamabad Capital Territory. Other major cities include Karachi, Lahore, Faisalabad, Rawalpindi, and Peshawar. Pakistan also has two autonomous regions: Gilgit-Baltistan and Azad Jammu and Kashmir. These regions are disputed by India and Pakistan over the Kashmir issue. Flag

The flag of Pakistan is a green field with a white crescent and star in the center, and a white vertical stripe at the hoist. The flag was adopted by the Constituent Assembly of Pakistan on 11 August 1947, just before the country gained independence from the British Empire. The flag represents the Islamic identity and heritage of Pakistan, as well as the diversity and unity of its people. The green color symbolizes prosperity and peace, the white color stands for all the minority religions in the country, the crescent signifies progress, and the star denotes knowledge and light. The flag is also mentioned in the third verse of Pakistan's national anthem, and is flown on several important occasions, such as Republic Day, Independence Day, and Defence Day.

Nuclear Power

Pakistan is the first Muslim majority country in the world to construct and operate civil nuclear power plants. The Pakistan Atomic Energy Commission (PAEC), the scientific and nuclear governmental agency, is solely responsible for operating these power plants. Pakistan began developing nuclear weapons in January 1972 under Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, who delegated the program to the Chairman of the PAEC Munir Ahmad Khan with a commitment to having the device ready by the end of 1976. Pakistan has five nuclear power plants with a total capacity of 1,430 MW, and three more under construction with a planned capacity of 3,930 MW.
