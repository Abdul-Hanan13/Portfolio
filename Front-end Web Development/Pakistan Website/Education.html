Overview

Education

Education in Pakistan is overseen by the Federal Ministry of Education and provincial governments. Article 25-A of the Constitution obligates the state to provide free and compulsory quality education to all children aged 5 to 16 years.

The education system is divided into six levels: preschool (age 3–5), primary (years 1–5), middle (years 6–8), high (years 9–10, SSC), intermediate (years 11–12, HSSC), and university programs. The Higher Education Commission (HEC), established in 2002, oversees all universities.

Ages 5 – 10

Primary Education

Only about 67.5% of Pakistani children complete primary school. Pre-school (ages 3–5) covers Play Group, Nursery, and Kindergarten. Students then progress through junior school (years 1–5), followed by middle school (years 6–8).

The eight commonly examined primary disciplines are Arts, Computer Studies & ICT, General Science, Modern Languages (Urdu, English), Mathematics, Religious Education, and Social Studies. Provincial languages — Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashto — may also be taught, as may foreign languages like Arabic, French, Chinese, and German.

A foundation for the nation: primary education shapes the literacy and numeracy skills of over 20 million children annually.
☪
Years 9 – 12

Secondary Education

Secondary education begins in year 9 and lasts four years. Students sit national examinations administered by regional Boards of Intermediate and Secondary Education (BISE). After year 10, successful students receive the Secondary School Certificate (Matric).

Curriculum includes electives (Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science) and compulsory subjects (Mathematics, English, Urdu, Islamiat, Pakistan Studies). Years 11–12 lead to the Higher Secondary School Certificate (HSSC / FSc / FA / ICS).

Alternatives include O/A Levels, IGCSE, AP programmes, and Technical Education pathways (Matric Tech, DAE) for vocational training.

☪
Higher Learning

Tertiary Education

As of 2007, 6% of Pakistanis (9% men, 3.5% women) were university graduates — with targets set to raise this to 15%. After HSSC, students may pursue professional degrees in engineering, medicine, law, pharmacy, nursing, veterinary sciences, or general degrees (BA, BSc, BCom, BBA).

Bachelor's degrees are offered as Pass (2 years) or Honours (4 years). Teacher education programs include Associate degrees, B.Ed (2–4 years), Master's and PhD qualifications. Pakistan produces about 445,000 university graduates and 25,000–30,000 computer science graduates per year.

Pakistan's Higher Education Commission, established in 2002, oversees all degree-awarding institutions — driving quality, research, and access to higher learning.
